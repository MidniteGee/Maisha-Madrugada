Why Conserve Turtles?

Understanding Critical Threats to Sea Turtles

Sea turtles are vital indicators of marine health. Protecting them means preserving our oceans, restoring coastal ecosystems, and securing local community livelihoods in Tiwi.

Sea turtles are highly endangered marine species. Protecting them is critical to preserving marine biodiversity and ensuring the balance of our coastal and oceanic ecosystems.

Nesting females and eggs face critical threats from poaching. We patrol the beaches every night to locate nesting mothers, take their biometrics, and relocate the eggs to our hatchery for 24/7 monitoring.

Marine plastic pollution severely impacts turtle health and survival. Ingestion of plastic debris and entanglement in ghost nets pose daily hazards to these sea animals.

Commercialization of beaches has resulted in a severe lack of safe nesting sites. Protecting these beaches ensures that turtles can continue to lay eggs undisturbed.

We build community capacity by training locals as sea turtle monitors and eco-guides. This establishes sustainable sea turtle-based tourism and entrepreneurial ventures for residents.

When working in wildlife protection, nature always surprises us. Because wildlife is unpredictable, we cannot say exactly what your experience will be, but we are sure that the magic of saving turtles will inspire you. Note on Seasonality: Sea turtle nesting and hatching occur in seasonal cycles, though exact nesting and hatching months are subject to annual environmental factors.

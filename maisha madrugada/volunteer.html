Become a Part of Something Meaningful

Why Join as a Volunteer?

Be part of a passionate team dedicated to saving endangered sea turtles in Tiwi, Kenya. As a volunteer, you will participate in the entire conservation process, from night patrols to hatchery monitoring, while supporting community development and experiencing the unique Kenyan culture.

  • Patrol nesting beaches during evening and night walks to locate female turtles and secure their nests
  • Relocate vulnerable eggs to the hatchery and participate in 24/7 monitoring shifts
  • Assist newly hatched baby sea turtles in reaching the ocean safely to maximize survival
  • Conduct daily beach litter cleanups to maintain clean nesting environments
  • Engage in community service at local schools and give conservation talks to hotel guests

Volunteers are key to the preservation of the sea turtle species native to Kenya's coast. Join us in making a real difference.

Life as a Volunteer

Daily Schedule & Work Hours

Volunteers work 6 to 8 hours per day, 6 days per week. Breakfast is served at 7:00, lunch is served at 12:00, and dinner is served at 20:00. The schedule for hatchery shifts and beach patrols varies depending on the nesting season.

Free Time & Recreation

In your free time, enjoy being located just 20 meters from the ocean. You can relax on Coconut Beach, play beach volleyball on our local court, or access the Dive Centre right at the lodge. The vibrant Diani Beach is also just 25 minutes away by Tuk-Tuk or Boda Boda.

Accommodation & Meals

Living Arrangements

Volunteers stay in comfortable hostel-style dormitories with bunk beds, separated by gender, with up to 4 people per room and shared attached bathrooms. Single private rooms equipped with air conditioning are also available for an additional price.

Meals & Dining

Three nutritious meals are provided daily, featuring typical local dishes such as rice and beans, spaghetti, Chapati, Samosas, and fresh fish. Filtered drinking water is always available on-site at the camp for volunteers. A restaurant and convenience store are also located nearby for additional purchases.

Program Fees & Duration

All rates are listed in USD. Program fees cover accommodation, meals, filtered water, and training.

  • 2 Weeks (Minimum Stay) $500
  • 3 Weeks $750
  • 4 Weeks $1,000
  • 5 Weeks $1,250
  • 9 Weeks $2,150
  • 12 Weeks $3,000

Essential Info & Packing

Medical & Health Preparations

All volunteers must have their own health insurance. Recommended vaccinations for Kenya include yellow fever, tetanus, Hepatitis A and B, typhoid, and rabies. It is highly recommended to bring anti-malarial medications, rehydration supplements, sunscreen, after-sun lotion, insect repellent, and a good reusable water bottle. Good, affordable medical care is available in-country if required.

Packing Essentials

Clothing: Lightweight waterproof rain jacket, T-shirts/vests, light long-sleeved sweatshirt/fleece, forest-suitable trousers, shorts, swimwear, sunhat, and sunglasses.
Footwear: Walking boots or sneakers, flip-flops, and reef shoes.
Gear & Tech: Waterproof day pack, dry bag/ziplock bags, towel or sarong, solar panel charger, battery pack, head torch (rechargeable), and a padlock.
Documents: Photocopies of passport, travel insurance, and dive qualifications.
Conservation Gear: Tempered glass snorkel mask, and open-heel dive fins with booties.

Sunscreen Note: Please bring water-resistant, coral-friendly sunscreen to protect the marine environment (we highly recommend proWin Sun protection).
